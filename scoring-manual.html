1. Getting Started

1.1 Logging In

Step 1: Open the Application

Navigate to the application URL in your web browser.

Step 2: Enter Your Group PIN

When prompted, enter the 4-digit PIN provided by your tournament organizer.

This PIN is unique to your group and allows you to enter scores.

Note: As a scorer, you can access the Leaderboard and Scoring tabs only.

2. Viewing the Leaderboard

2.1 Understanding the Leaderboard

What You'll See:

  • Player Rankings: Current standings sorted by net score (lowest is best)
  • Handicap: Each player's handicap
  • Playing Handicap: Adjusted handicap based on course difficulty
  • Gross Total: Actual strokes taken
  • Net Total: Gross score minus handicap strokes
  • Stableford Points: Points earned (more is better)
Winners:
üèÜ Medal Play Winner: Player with the lowest net score
ü•á Stableford Winner: Player with the most Stableford points

2.2 Expanding Scorecards

View Hole-by-Hole Scores:

  1. Click on any player row in the leaderboard
  2. The row expands to show their complete scorecard
  3. See scores for all 18 holes with color coding
  4. View Front 9, Back 9, and total scores

3. Entering Scores

3.1 Accessing Score Entry

Steps:

  1. Click on the SCORING tab
  2. Your group will be automatically selected
  3. You'll see all players in your group listed

3.2 Entering Scores for Each Hole

How to Enter Scores:

  1. Find the player's name in the left column
  2. For each hole (1-18), enter the number of strokes taken
  3. Press Enter or Tab to move to the next hole
  4. Scores are automatically saved as you type
‚úì Auto-Save Feature: Scores save automatically - no save button needed!

3.3 Understanding the Scoring Grid

What You'll See:

The scoring grid is divided into two sections:

  • Front 9 (Holes 1-9): First half of the course
  • Back 9 (Holes 10-18): Second half of the course

Each section shows:

  • Hole numbers
  • Par for each hole
  • Stroke Index (SI) - hole difficulty ranking
  • Input boxes for gross scores
  • Calculated net scores (gross minus handicap strokes)
  • Stableford points earned

3.4 Marking NR (No Return)

If a Player Doesn't Complete a Hole:

  1. Enter 0 (zero) in the score box for that hole
  2. The system will mark it as "NR" (No Return)
  3. NR holes:
    • Will not count towards gross/net totals
    • Will show as "NR" in totals if any NR exists
    • Still calculate Stableford points (0 points for NR)
Example: If a player picks up their ball on hole 7, enter "0" for that hole.

4. Understanding Score Colors

4.1 Color-Coded Scoring

Scores are color-coded to show performance relative to par:

Score Type Color Meaning
Eagle or Better Gold background 2 or more strokes under par
Birdie Light green background 1 stroke under par
Par White background Equal to par
Bogey Light orange background 1 stroke over par
Double Bogey+ Light red background 2 or more strokes over par

5. Understanding Handicap Calculations

5.1 How Handicaps Work

Playing Handicap:

The system automatically calculates each player's playing handicap based on:

  • Player's handicap index
  • Course slope rating
  • Course rating

Stroke Index (SI):

Each hole has a Stroke Index from 1-18:

  • SI 1: Hardest hole on the course
  • SI 18: Easiest hole on the course
  • Players receive handicap strokes on holes based on their playing handicap

Example:

If a player has a playing handicap of 12:

  • They receive 1 stroke on holes with SI 1-12
  • No strokes on holes with SI 13-18
  • This means their net score = gross score - 1 on those 12 holes

5.2 Stableford Scoring

Points System:

Stableford points are awarded based on net score relative to par:

Net Score Points
Double Eagle or better 5 points
Eagle (-2) 4 points
Birdie (-1) 3 points
Par (0) 2 points
Bogey (+1) 1 point
Double Bogey or worse (+2 or more) 0 points
Remember: These points are based on NET score (after handicap strokes are applied), not gross score.

7. Troubleshooting

Issue Solution
Scores not saving Check your internet connection. Scores auto-save every few seconds when connected.
Can't see my group Make sure you entered the correct group PIN. Try logging out and back in.
Wrong score entered Simply click on the score box and enter the correct number. It will save automatically.
Leaderboard not updating Data refreshes every 5 seconds. If it still doesn't update, check your internet connection.
Forgot group PIN Contact your tournament organizer for the PIN.
